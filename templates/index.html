Standardized Precipitation Index (SPI) :The Standardized Precipitation Index (SPI) has attracted significant attention in drought research (Vicente et al., 2010). It is calculated by taking the logarithm of the ratio between precipitation and long-term average rainfall within the study region over one year. The resulting SPI values were then normalized to -2 to +2, where -2 indicated extreme drought and +2 indicated extreme wetness.

Normalized Difference Vegitation Index(NDVI), Enhanced Vegetation Index(EVI), Normalized Difference Water Index(NDWI)

  • NDVI is a spectrally derived index of plant biomass that is often used to measure agricultural drought conditions.
  • EVI is utilized in the present research to estimate the severity of drought by using satellite data to measure the amount of vegetation contribution for drought indices, the temperature of the surface, and the health of the vegetation
  • NDWI is known to be strongly related to the plant water content. It is therefore a very good proxy for plant water stress.
