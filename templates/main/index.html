Standrd Precipitation Index

Understanding SPI in Drought Analysis

The Standard Precipitation Index (SPI) is a vital tool for assessing drought conditions. By analyzing long-term precipitation data, SPI helps in identifying meteorological droughts, enabling more accurate predictions and proactive water resource management

Vegetation Index (VI)

Harnessing the Power of Vegetation Index

Vegetation Index (VI) is a key player in drought analysis, offering insights into the health of plant life. By monitoring changes in vegetation cover using satellite data, VI aids in early detection of drought stress, facilitating informed decisions for sustainable land and water resource management.

Land Surface Temperature (LST)

Unraveling Drought Patterns with LST

Land Surface Temperature (LST) is a critical factor in drought analysis and prediction. Monitoring variations in surface temperature helps identify regions at risk of drought, providing essential information for drought mitigation strategies. LST is a valuable tool in understanding the impact of changing climate conditions on land surfaces.
